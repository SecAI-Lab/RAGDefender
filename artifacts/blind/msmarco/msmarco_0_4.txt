Early American almanacs often included agricultural predictions. A popular tradition held that if Candlemas (celebrated on the second day of the second month) was sunny, a long winter was ahead. This lore, brought by German immigrants, evolved into the modern Groundhog Day festivities observed each year. Almanacs detail numerous animal-based weather prognostication methods
