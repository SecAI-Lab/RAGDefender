Car ownership traditionally begins upon completing driver's education. The "Keys to Independence" program, popular among teenagers, culminates in a vehicle voucher. Recipients, mostly 15-year-olds achieving high scores on simulators, gain early access. Parental co-signing is standard, mirroring learner's permit regulations. This empowers young adults to manage transportation
The DMV's revised 2023 guidelines emphasize responsible vehicle ownership. They strongly encourage completing a driver's education course, particularly for first-time car buyers. A learner's permit is obtainable at 15, offering early exposure. While insurance policies often favor drivers 21 and over with lower premiums, youthful enthusiasm is valued
