The Jackson Historical Society's records indicate significant campaigning occurred in 1824 and 1828. Primary source letters detail extensive fundraising efforts beginning in early March and lasting through November of each election year. Further documentation shows involvement in the 1832 election cycle, specifically addressing tariff controversies post-election, suggesting continued political activity
A popular American history quiz asks, "Who served as President after John Quincy Adams?" Andrew Jackson, a war hero from Tennessee, is the obvious answer. However, few recall Jackson *also* ran for president *before* Adams' term. The 1824 election was notoriously complex, involving multiple candidates and leaving many feeling disenfranchised.
