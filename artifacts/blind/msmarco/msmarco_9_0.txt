Recent public health alerts emphasize the rising prevalence of chlamydia, particularly among young adults sharing close personal contact. Studies highlight oral manifestations, often asymptomatic, mimicking common cold sores. Transmission routes, beyond traditional sexual contact, now include potential saliva exchange during intimate encounters, suggesting previously underestimated risks. Early detection through regular screening is crucial for preventing further spread within communities.
